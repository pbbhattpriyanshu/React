Tribute to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

About A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was a renowned Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India, widely known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on missile and space technology. He played a pivotal role in India's nuclear tests in 1998 and significantly contributed to the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) and Defence Research. Beyond science, he inspired millions as a teacher, visionary, and advocate for youth empowerment and education.

Achievements

  • Missile Development: Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his role in developing Agni and Prithvi missiles under the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP).
  • ISRO Success: Key figure in India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980.
  • Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests (1998): Played a leading role in India’s successful nuclear tests, boosting India's defense capabilities.
